Ortakent is a settlement in the central part of Bodrum, with a calm sea and a wide sandy beach. It stands out especially for families with small children thanks to its safe beach layout and crowd-free atmosphere. In spring the sea water is still cool, but it's a perfect time for green spaces, cycling paths and outdoor play activities. Let's look at what you can do in this quiet area, 20 minutes from central Bodrum and 15 minutes from Turgutreis.

## 1. Safe Sea Fun at Ortakent Yahşi Beach

Ortakent's greatest advantage is its 3-kilometre-long fine sandy shore. Compared with other coves, the sea here is shallower and free of waves. Ideal for small children: even after walking 10–15 metres into the sea, the water stays at waist level.

**Spring tip**: In April and May the sea water is 18–20 degrees, cold for swimming but great for paddling and playing in the sand. Between 09:00 and 11:00 in the morning the sun is gentle and children can move around comfortably.

**Bring with you**:
- A beach umbrella (tree shade along the shore is limited)
- Water shoes (some areas have small pebbles)
- Sand toys (the wide beach suits every age)

There are local shops and markets all along the beach, so you can cover your water and fruit needs there.

## 2. Rent a Bike and Tour the Coastal Road

The coastal strip between Ortakent and Turgutreis is fairly flat and safe for cycling. At the area's bike rental points you can find models with a child seat or a side basket.

**Suggested route**:
- **Start**: Ortakent centre
- **Destination**: towards Turgutreis Marina (5 km, about 25 minutes of pedalling)
- **Stopover**: Akyarlar area (there's a green space for a break)

In spring the air temperature is between 20 and 25 degrees, so cycling won't tire the children out. Between 16:00 and 18:00 the sun softens, making it the ideal time slot.

**A small note**: For children under 5, a rear-mounted seat is best, while children aged 6–10 can ride their own bike.

## 3. Ortakent Market and Farmers' Market Trip

The local market set up every Tuesday is a fun experience for children to get to know seasonal fruit and observe local culture. Fresh strawberries (late April–May), apricots (early June) and various plums are plentiful in spring.

**Tips for the market with kids**:
1. Go between 09:00 and 10:00 in the morning (you can stroll comfortably before the crowds)
2. Give your child a small basket so they can pick their own fruit
3. Try the freshly squeezed orange juice stalls
4. Take a look at the handmade toy and wooden goods stalls

The market area is in central Ortakent, with free parking available.

## 4. Nature Walk at Pedasa Ancient City

Located on the hills right behind Ortakent, Pedasa offers both history and a nature walk. A short trek among the ruins gives children a taste of adventure.

**Walk details**:
- **Distance**: 2 km (round trip)
- **Duration**: 45–60 minutes (including photo stops)
- **Difficulty**: Easy/moderate (there are some stone steps)
- **Age recommendation**: children over 5 walk it comfortably

**Bring with you**:
- Plenty of water (none sold along the way)
- A hat and sunscreen
- Light snacks (for children's energy)

In spring the lush vegetation and wildflowers make the walk colourful. The early morning hours (08:00–10:00) are cool and crowd-free.

## 5. Stand-Up Paddle (SUP) and Kayak Experience

Ortakent's calm sea is perfect for an introduction to water sports. At the area's water sports centres you can rent a SUP or kayak with a child's life jacket and a guide.

**Recommendations by age group**:
- **Ages 4–7**: a two-person kayak with a parent (balance is easy)
- **Ages 8+**: can try their own SUP (in the shallows, life jacket mandatory)

**Safety notes**:
1. Always wear a life jacket (included in the rental price)
2. Stay within 50 metres of the shore (there's no current, but it's a precaution)
3. Between 10:00 and 12:00 the wind is light, the ideal time

Water sports centre guides are generally experienced with children, and a 15–20 minute trial session may be free.

## 6. Children's Playgrounds and Green Spaces

In central Ortakent there are small parks arranged by the municipality. Swings, slides and climbing units suit ages 2–10.

**Standout parks**:
- **Ortakent Central Park**: shaded, with benches, lively between 18:00 and 20:00 in the evening
- **Yahşi Neighbourhood Park**: smaller but close to the sea (200 metres), with picnic tables

In spring the evening hours are cool and children can play comfortably. If you bring simple toys like a ball or a jump rope, you'll have an enjoyable time.

## 7. Kite Flying (with the Spring Breezes)

In April and May a light breeze blows along Ortakent beach. Ideal conditions for flying a kite. You can find colourful kites at local shops and fly them in the wide open space on the beach.

**Kite-flying tips**:
- The wind is most stable between 15:00 and 17:00
- Choose the empty parts of the beach (away from other families)
- Let your child hold the string and help them run to lift the kite into the air

Simple kites are inexpensive — a wonderful spring memory for children.

## Accommodation and Practical Information in Ortakent

**Family apartment/guesthouse suggestions**:
Boutique apartments and family guesthouses are abundant in Ortakent. Options with a kitchen, within a 5–10 minute walk of the sea, offer budget-friendly solutions. Especially in spring (April–May), prices are noticeably lower than in the summer season.

**Shops/essentials**: The area has local markets, a pharmacy and a health centre. Bodrum State Hospital is 20 minutes away.

**Transport**: 20 minutes from central Bodrum by minibus, 15 minutes by private car. There's no parking problem.

**Restaurants/cafés**: There are family-friendly venues all along the seafront, with some offering a children's menu and play area. There's a wide range of local fish restaurants and pizza/pasta options.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Ortakent beach safe for small children?**  
Yes, the sea is very shallow and free of waves. Even after walking 5–10 metres, the water stays at waist level. The sand is soft with minimal pebbles. Water shoes are still recommended.

**Can you swim in spring?**  
In April and May the water temperature is around 18–20 degrees, which is cold for swimming. However, paddling, playing in the sand and water sports (SUP/kayaking) can be done comfortably. The water starts warming up in early June.

**Are there child-friendly restaurants in Ortakent?**  
Yes, there are family-friendly venues all along the seafront. Most offer a children's menu, food warming and a play area. Local fish restaurants and pizza/pasta options are plentiful.

**What are the bike rental prices like?**  
Hourly and daily rentals are reasonably priced. Models with a child seat or side basket cost a small extra fee. In spring there are sometimes discounts on daily rentals.

**Is it easy to get from Ortakent to central Bodrum?**  
Very easy. Minibuses leave every 15–20 minutes and the trip takes 20 minutes. By private car it's 15 minutes. Taxis are also a reasonable option.
