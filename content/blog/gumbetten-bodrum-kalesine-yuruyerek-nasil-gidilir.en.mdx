## How Many Minutes Does It Take from Gümbet to Bodrum Castle

Starting from Gümbet beach, the walk to Bodrum Castle takes an average of 30–40 minutes. In spring, since the weather is neither too hot nor too cold, this route is ideal for families. If you're walking with your children, count on a total of 50 minutes including breaks.

The distance is roughly 2.5 kilometres. As you'll be walking with a sea view the whole way, it's both a practical and pleasant transport option.

## Which Route Should You Follow

1. **Start from Gümbet beach**: If you're staying in a hotel or apartment, head towards the seafront. The Gümbet coastal road is clearly marked.
2. **Pass the Bar Street**: Follow Gümbet's main avenue (Adnan Menderes Bulvarı), walking parallel to the sea.
3. **Continue from Bardakçı Cove**: After Gümbet you'll reach Bardakçı, a small cove. This point means you're halfway.
4. **Cross Kumbahçe beach**: After Bardakçı you'll come out onto Kumbahçe seafront. This is very close to central Bodrum.
5. **Enter Bodrum Marina**: After Kumbahçe you'll enter the marina, and the castle is now within sight.
6. **Walk to the castle**: From the marina, there's a 5-minute walk left to the castle. Follow the signs and you won't have any trouble.

## What's Along the Way

The walking route runs along the seafront, so children can keep going without getting bored. You can take a short break at Bardakçı Cove and Kumbahçe.

- **Spring advantage**: Between March and May the sun isn't harsh, and walking between 09:00 and 11:00 in the morning is very comfortable. The afternoon is possible too, but it can be a little hotter.
- **For families with children**: The road is flat and paved, so you can take a pushchair. However, the pavements may be narrow in some spots, so take care.
- **Water and snacks**: There are shops along the way, but keep a water bottle with you. Bring fruit or biscuits for the children.

## Which Hours Are Better for Walking

In spring, the early morning hours are the most ideal time. Between 08:00 and 10:00 the air is cool and the route isn't crowded. If you'd rather walk in the afternoon, go after 16:00 — the view at sunset is magnificent.

Since weekends can be crowded, we recommend walking on a weekday. Especially in April the locals also go for walks, so there's a friendly atmosphere.

## Things to Watch Out for While Walking

1. **Comfortable shoes**: Wear trainers or walking shoes. We don't recommend flip-flops, as your feet may start to ache.
2. **Sunscreen**: The sun beats down even in spring, so apply it especially to children's faces and arms.
3. **Hat or cap**: Protect your head; a long walk can cause a headache.
4. **Traffic**: There's vehicle traffic when leaving Gümbet, so hold the children's hands.
5. **Rest points**: At Bardakçı or Kumbahçe there are benches where you can sit and look out at the sea, so take a break if the children get tired.

## How to Make the Return Trip

After visiting the castle, you can walk back along the same route. But if the children are tired, use the minibus. There are direct lines to Gümbet from the minibus stops in central Bodrum, and the fare per person is very low. You'll reach Gümbet in 10–15 minutes.

Alternatively, you can find a taxi around the marina; ask the price in advance.

## Castle Entry and Visiting Information

Bodrum Castle is open to visitors every day of the week (it may be closed on Mondays, so check beforehand). There is an entrance fee, with a discount for children. Inside there's the Museum of Underwater Archaeology, a lovely experience for history-loving families.

There are areas in the castle garden where you can sit and the children can run and play. Plan on 1–1.5 hours for the museum tour.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is walking from Gümbet to Bodrum Castle tiring?**  
No, the route is flat and runs along the seafront. At a moderate pace it takes 30–40 minutes, and families with children can do it comfortably.

**Can you do the walk with a pushchair?**  
Yes, the road is paved and flat. The pavement may be narrow in some spots, but in general it's possible to take a pushchair.

**Which season is better for walking?**  
Spring (March–May) and autumn (September–October) are ideal. It can get very hot in summer, so go in the early morning.

**What should we do for toilets along the way?**  
There are cafés on Bardakçı and Kumbahçe seafronts that you can use when needed. As you get closer to central Bodrum you'll find more options.

**Where do you catch the minibus back to Gümbet?**  
There are direct lines to Gümbet from the minibus stops in central Bodrum. You'll find them easily around the marina, and they run frequently.
