## How to Get from Bodrum Airport to the Centre

There are four main ways to get from Bodrum Airport to the centre and the holiday areas: private transfer, HAVAŞ/bus, taxi and car rental. Milas-Bodrum Airport is about 35 km from the centre and takes around 40–50 minutes by car. Which method makes sense for you depends on the area you're heading to, the size of your group and your holiday style.

To avoid the "now how do I get there?" stress after landing, it's best to plan your transport in advance. Below we cover each option with its advantages, distance and time estimates, and which area it suits.

## Distances and Times: A Look at the Areas

Milas-Bodrum Airport lies in the east of the peninsula. The approximate distances to the areas and estimated times by car in normal traffic are as follows:

- **Central Bodrum**: ~35 km, ~40–50 minutes
- **Gümbet**: ~38 km, ~45–55 minutes
- **Bitez**: ~40 km, ~50 minutes
- **Ortakent**: ~42 km, ~50–60 minutes
- **Gündoğan**: ~45 km, ~55–65 minutes
- **Turgutreis**: ~50 km, ~60–70 minutes
- **Yalıkavak**: ~50 km, ~60–70 minutes
- **Torba**: ~30 km, ~35–45 minutes

These times are estimates; they can stretch when summer-season and weekend traffic is heavy. Factor in this difference when heading to the outlying areas.

## 1. Private Transfer: Door-to-Door Comfort

A private transfer is a service arranged in advance around your flight time that takes you straight from the airport to your apartment door. No changeovers, no waiting, no hunting for the address.

**Who is it sensible for?**

- Families with children (you can request a child seat and won't change over with luggage)
- Passengers landing late (the vehicle waits for you)
- Those heading to outlying areas like Yalıkavak, Turgutreis and Gündoğan
- Larger groups with a lot of suitcases

It's the smoothest option for late arrivals and the peak season. Simply arrange it by sharing your flight details in advance. [You can review our private transfer service here.](/vip-transfer)

## 2. HAVAŞ and the Bus: The Economical Option

HAVAŞ services are a public transport service planned around flight arrivals, connecting the airport to central Bodrum. It suits passengers who prioritise budget and travel with light luggage.

**Things to note:**

- HAVAŞ usually drops off at the central bus station; from there you may need additional transport (a dolmuş minibus or taxi) to your apartment.
- If you're heading to an outlying area you'll need to change over; this can be tiring with children and heavy luggage.
- Service times vary with flight density; check the service time against your landing time in advance.

The dolmuş minibuses within Bodrum also run frequently between the centre and the areas; once you reach the centre you can switch to a dolmuş to your area.

## 3. Taxi: Fast but Independent

It's possible to find a taxi at the airport exit. It's practical for small groups who don't want a changeover and want to go directly.

**Practical notes:**

- Over long distances (Yalıkavak, Turgutreis) the time increases; factor this in.
- There may be waiting in the peak season and at night; that's why a pre-arranged transfer is often more predictable.
- Before setting off, confirm the address you're going to and the approximate distance with the driver.

## 4. Car Rental: Freedom Throughout the Holiday

If you want to explore different coves, beaches and markets on your own schedule throughout your holiday, renting a car is the freest option. The Bodrum peninsula is large, and the distances between areas are far easier to cover by car.

**Who is it sensible for?**

- Those planning to visit several areas and beaches
- Those who want to go to spots like Turgutreis market and Gümüşlük
- Those who want to complement the flexibility of an apartment stay with a car

Ask in advance whether your apartment has parking; in the summer season, parking can be limited in some central areas. [You can see the car rental options here.](/arac-kiralama)

## Which Makes Sense for Which Area?

- **Central Bodrum, Gümbet, Torba**: The areas closest to the airport. Taxi, HAVAŞ or private transfer; all are practical.
- **Bitez, Ortakent**: Mid-distance. A private transfer is comfortable for families with children, and a rental car is ideal for those wanting independence.
- **Gündoğan, Yalıkavak, Turgutreis**: Outlying areas. A private transfer for changeover-free comfort; a rental car stands out if you plan to explore the surroundings.

## Comparing the Options at a Glance

To quickly see which suits you, let's line up the four options side by side:

- **Private transfer**: High comfort, no changeover, safe on late arrivals. You reach your apartment directly. The most comfortable option for families with children and larger groups.
- **HAVAŞ / bus**: Economical, practical with light luggage. Usually drops off at the centre; outlying areas need a changeover. Checking service times in advance is essential.
- **Taxi**: Fast and direct, suitable for small groups. There may be waiting in busy periods; over long distances the time increases.
- **Car rental**: Freedom throughout the holiday; ideal for those wanting to visit several areas. You need to check the parking situation in advance.

There's no single right answer; the decision depends on the area you're heading to, the size of your group and your holiday style.

## Tips for Families with Children and Heavy Luggage

If you're travelling with a child or many suitcases, a transport plan makes the first hour of the holiday far easier. To avoid the rush of hunting for a seat at the airport, waiting for a dolmuş or finding the address after your flight, here are a few practical suggestions:

- **Go door to door**: Changing over with a pushchair, a baby bag and suitcases is tiring. A transfer that drops you straight at your apartment removes the stress of the first day.
- **Request a child seat in advance**: State your need for a baby or child seat when booking; it arrives ready on arrival.
- **Share your arrival time**: By providing your flight number in advance, you can ensure the vehicle waits for you in case of delays.
- **Keep water and snacks handy**: The road to outlying areas can take an hour; a little preparation for the children is a good idea.

## Practical Tips for Airport Transport

- Confirm your flight time and apartment address in advance; arrange the transfer up front, especially for late arrivals.
- If travelling with a child, state your need for a baby/child seat in advance.
- Traffic gets heavier in the summer season; leave a margin in your time estimates.
- If you have a lot of luggage, confirm the vehicle capacity in advance.
- Plan your return transfer up front too; decide the departure time together based on your flight time.

## Conclusion

For transport from Bodrum Airport, the right option varies with the area you're heading to and your holiday style. A private transfer stands out for door-to-door comfort and peace of mind on late arrivals; HAVAŞ and the bus for budget; a rental car for an independent holiday.
